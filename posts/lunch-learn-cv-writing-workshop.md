## Get the basics right

* Make your name clear
* Contact details are included
* Sections are clearly labelled
* Check for errors
* Get someone to review if possible



## Basic Section

* Contact details
* Personal statement
* Skills
* Work experience
* Interests
* References



## Personal statement

* Talk about the future - what you want
* Be positive
* Be succinct



## Make it easy to read

* It is vital that the CV is easy to read
* Don't have endless blocks of text, try to be succinct and make important information easy to spot
* Use clear fonts
* Use bullet points



## Include examples

* As a tech recruiter or employer, one of the first things that would be looked at is example works
* Give a link to HTML - CSS and other projects or code



## Skills

* Make the most of the skills you have
* Make them jump out a potential employers
* Along with examples of work the skills are what people will be looking for





## Skill top tips

* Don't oversell
* Don't add too many
* Try to include experience level of skills



## Work experience

* Don't include it all unless it's relevant
* Talk about transferable skills
* Key duties, not the whole job spec



## Think about style

* First of all, as we have already said it is important for the CV to be clear however, if possible try to look at the style of a cv to make it stand out there are many templates out there that can be used to create "styled" CV's



## Tailored CV's

* Tweak your cv depending on the role you are applying for



## Cover letter

* Try and add a cover letter if you can, but tweak it to the role you are applying for.
